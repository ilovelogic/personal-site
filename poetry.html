Seeing with heart & soul

Here are four short poems that are an invitation to wonder and ponder.

Full of nourishment, I no longer beg for poison

When you can see the stars, you don't need to sell yourself for candle sticks
When you are warmed in a loving embrace, you need not lay on the ground by the half holding on embers
And when your heart and mind reflect on God, there is no reason to entertain them with Earth's passing tricks
For the sweet Spring and Summer within yourself keep you at peace during all cold, cruel decembers

The Blind See the Sky

“There are no stars tonight,"
Proclaims the man with His eyes closed
While even the ones born without sight
Can sense the wonder that the transcendentals once composed

Locking Out the Light

There is a jar within you meant to be a home for the fireflies
And they flitter about, in search of the opening to your light-waiting case
Will you lock it away so that both you and the fluttering joys must agonize
Or will you lift your jar to the sky by choosing to embrace the potential of grace?

Offspring of the Hope for Shared Joy

The crowned king of infinite power exists in the art of ballet
His joy in the motion is so strong that He begs me that He might convey
The beauty of dance to I, His stumbling child and potentialized day
The play of His atoms is wonder and still He calls my moves His sunray

A. Kelley · School Email · CSE 134B, Summer 2026
